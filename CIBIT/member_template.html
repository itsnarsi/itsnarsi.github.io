BIO

Dr. Reza R. Derakhshani is an Associate Professor of Computer Science and Electrical Engineering at University of Missouri, Kansas City (UMKC). He joined UMKC’s School of Computing and Engineering in 2004 as an Assistant Professor. He earned his Ph.D. and Master’s degrees in Computer Engineering and Electrical Engineering respectively from West Virginia University, and his bachelor’s degree in Electrical Engineering from Iran’s University of Science and Technology. Dr. Derakhshani's research interests are in biometrics (physical and psychophysiological), biomedical signal and image processing, and physiological system identification using computational intelligence paradigms and novel hardware platforms. His interdisciplinary research has brought about close collaborations with various researchers and institutions. His recent research projects include a new patented biometric modality based on vasculature on the white of the eye, hyperfocal ocular-facial biometrics, postmortem ocular biometrics, study of balance in Parkinson’s patients, data-driven modeling of human joints, noninvasive brain computer interfacing and brain state monitoring, and non-obtrusive affective-cognitive analysis using postural and ocular dynamics for credibility assessment. His work has been by various state and federal agencies as well as private industry, and has resulted in a number of peer-reviewed publications, a granted U.S. patent, and three pending patent applications. He is also the Chief Scientist at EyeVerify LLC, a local startup based on his patented novel “EyeVein” biometrics.
